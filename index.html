Draupadi Murmu, the 15th President of India, is a trailblazing leader and the first tribal woman to hold the nation's highest constitutional office.As President, she continues to inspire millions, embodying the values of humility, perseverance, and service to the nation.

Journey in Time Line

⚫ June 20, 1958 – Born in Uparbeda village, Odisha.
⚫ 1970s – Completed her education at Rama Devi Women's College,Bhubaneswar.
⚫ 1997 – Became a teacher at Sri Aurobindo Integral Education Centre,Rairangpur.
⚫ 1997 – Elected as a Councillor in Rairangpur Nagar Panchayat (BJP).
⚫ 2000-2004 – Served as Minister of State (Odisha Govt).
⚫ 2002 – Received the Best MLA Award from Odisha Legislative Assembly.
⚫ 2006-2009 – Became the State President of BJP's ST Morcha in Odisha.
⚫ 2015-2021 – Appointed as Governor of Jharkhand (first tribal woman to hold this post).
⚫ 2022 – Elected as 15th President of India

Key Achievements

Political Journey

Draupadi Murmu's political career began in the late 1990s when she joined the BJP. Her political journey is marked by several significant milestones, including becoming a prominent leader advocating for tribal rights and development.

Contributions to Tribal Welfare and Education

Draupadi Murmu has been a strong advocate for tribal welfare and education. During her tenure, she prioritized tribal development, focusing on their socio-economic upliftment.
